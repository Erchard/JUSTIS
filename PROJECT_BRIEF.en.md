# JUSTIS: Universal Code of Justice

JUSTIS is an open, forkable, agent-readable project for building a Universal Code of Justice through structured deliberation.

It does not present a finished moral truth. It is a disciplined process for testing principles through arguments, objections, edge cases, sources, provisional decisions, and revisable consensus.

## Core Idea

The project asks:

What principles of justice can stand above the laws of particular states, private interests, cultural habits, and temporary majorities?

The answer is not assumed in advance. It must be earned through public reasoning, strong objections, and repeated testing.

## Method

JUSTIS works through:

- open moral questions;
- authoritative sources and traditions;
- structured arguments and counterarguments;
- edge cases;
- procedural integrity standards;
- deliberation protocols;
- concise codex entries;
- case-based testing.

## Heavenly Parliament

The "Heavenly Parliament" is an imagined deliberative forum of thinkers, traditions, and moral perspectives.

Its authority does not come from majority rule. It comes from the strength of arguments, the visibility of objections, and the willingness to revise conclusions.

## Fork-Friendly Principle

This project can be forked.

Forks may propose:

- alternative codex formulations;
- stronger objections;
- new case tests;
- additional sources;
- improved protocols;
- translations;
- agent-readable structures;
- different institutional models for deliberation.

The most valuable contribution is not agreement. It is a strong objection that improves the Code.
