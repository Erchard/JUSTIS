# JUSTIS Project Brief

JUSTIS is an open project for building a Universal Code of Justice through structured moral deliberation.

The project is designed as a public ethical infrastructure rather than a finished doctrine. Its central rule is simple: a principle should not enter the Code unless it can survive strong objections, difficult cases, and traceable deliberation.

## Public Value

JUSTIS may be useful to:

- philosophers and ethicists who want a structured corpus of moral argument;
- legal thinkers who want to compare positive law with deeper principles of justice;
- AI agents that need a transparent format for moral reasoning;
- translators and researchers who want to test whether a principle survives across languages and traditions;
- critics who want a place where strong objections are preserved rather than suppressed.

## Current Product

The canonical Code is intentionally concise and written in Latin:

- `codex/UNIVERSAL_JUSTICE_CODE.md`

Official translations are stored in:

- `codex/translations/`

Supporting evidence is kept outside the final Code:

- `support/codex/`
- `protocols/`
- `arguments/`
- `research/`
- `cases/`

## Contribution Invitation

Bring the strongest objection you can find. If the objection is stronger than the current principle, the Code should change.
